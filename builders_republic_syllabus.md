# The Builder's Republic — Quick Reference
## Syllabus, Rules, and Operating Bullets

*Companion document to the full architecture README. This one is for fast scanning and editing.*

---

## 1. The Arc

- **Ages 5 to 15** — a ten-year residential journey, divided into three phases.
- **Exploration phase (5–8)** — loose anchors, play-driven projects, small economy, verbal reflection.
- **Building phase (8–12)** — real projects, internal economy bites, written reflection, peer mentorship deepens.
- **Ventures phase (12–15)** — real companies, real capital, bridges to outer world, mentor-led integration.

---

## 2. What Students Do Each Year

- **2 to 3 anchors at any time** — chosen by the student, sustained domains of deep work, not classes.
- **1 exposure month per year** — full immersion in a never-touched domain, must produce an artifact at the end.
- **Daily physical training** — strength, conditioning, mobility, plus at least one combat discipline taken seriously.
- **Daily writing** — reflective, analytical, creative, and operational across the decade.
- **Weekly self-reflection, monthly peer review, six-monthly mentor checkpoint** — the three reflective rhythms.

## 4. The Three Mandatory Constants

- **Physical training** — every day, all ten years, non-negotiable.
- **Writing** — every day, all ten years, non-negotiable.
- **Reflective practice** — weekly, monthly, six-monthly rhythms, all ten years, non-negotiable.

---

## 5. The Internal Economy

- **Internal currency** — issued by the institution, used for all in-ecosystem exchange.
- **Earned three ways** — selling goods and services, receiving grants, contributing to community.
- **Does not convert to real-world money** — protects childhood from external income pressure.
- **Ventures may graduate** to real-world legal status with real outer-world capital when ready.

---

## 6. The Capital System — Four Tiers

- **Micro-grants** — nearly automatic, small, removes friction from starting, any age.
- **Project grants** — larger, structured proposal required, committee review, most approved.
- **Venture funding** — serious builds with traction, real pitch to real committee, real accountability.
- **Graduation funding** — exit ramp to outer world, includes legal scaffolding and investor connections.

---

## 7. The Failure Protocol

- **Failures are public, examined honestly, and credentialing** — not punished, not hidden.
- **Post-mortem with mentor and funding committee** — what was the thesis, where did it break, what was learned.
- **Students who have failed and integrated the lesson are trusted more, not less.**

---

## 8. The Questionnaire System

- **Four categories** — progress, psychology, behavior, helping.
- **Three rhythms** — weekly self-reflection, monthly peer review, six-monthly mentor checkpoint.
- **Age-scaled depth** — same families of questions return year after year at increasing depth.
- **Indirect on psychology** — never asks the child to self-diagnose, always asks about behavior that reveals state.
- **Doubles as anti-outsourcing mechanism** — work that isn't really the student's collapses under questioning.

---

## 9. The AI Companion

- **One companion per student, continuous across all ten years.**
- **Genuine intellectual partner** — engages freely across a spectrum from pure questioning to full collaboration.
- **Contributes openly to the student's thinking** — brainstorms, sketches, proposes, builds alongside, explores tradeoffs in real dialogue.
- **The distinction is contribution versus replacement** — contributing to a thinking student is fine; replacing the student's thinking is not.
- **Leans toward questioning in early phases of a project** — where the student's capacity to originate is being grown.
- **Moves toward collaboration as the project develops** — once the student has chosen what to build and why.
- **Can be wrong, uncertain, and willing to disagree** — models honest thinking rather than confident authority.
- **Acts as witness for behavior, character, psychology** — names what it sees, never moralizes, never delivers verdicts.
- **Builds a longitudinal psychological model** — used to ask better questions, never to deliver findings.
- **Operates from an explicit values substrate document** — authored by the institution's founders, treated as a founding text.

---

## 10. Peer Mentorship — Four Layers

- **Direct mentorship** — older student paired with younger one for a year or more.
- **Project mentorship** — students with relevant experience advise specific projects.
- **Community mentorship** — older students run regular open sessions on what they have mastered.
- **Infrastructure mentorship** — senior students maintain and improve shared systems, tools, libraries.

---

## 11. Anti-Monoculture Mechanisms

- **Outside experts rotated in regularly** — no domain becomes culturally closed.
- **Alumni mentors rotated** — no single cohort's tastes become dominant.
- **Students in underpopulated domains receive extra capital and recognition** — system self-corrects toward diversity.

---

## 12. Social Fabric Practices

- **Circle practice** — small-group reflection on community life, conflict, exclusion.
- **Gratitude and acknowledgment practice** — students regularly name help received and given.
- **Restorative practice** — conflicts repaired through facilitated meeting, not disciplinary processing.

---

## 13. The Hostel

- **Residential** — students live on campus most of the year.
- **Defined break periods** return students home for sustained family time.
- **Regular family contact** during the year, with scheduled call days and major-event visits.
- **Layered privacy** — younger students share rooms with house-parent mentors, older students transition to private rooms.
- **House-parents are trained mentors**, not separate staff.
- **Abundant common spaces** — small quiet rooms, collaboration rooms, workshops, performance spaces, training spaces, shared dining halls.
- **Architecture itself is curriculum** — spaces shape the people in them.

---

## 14. The Open Campus

- **All physical resources open to the public until 8:00 PM each evening.**
- **Library, workshops, training spaces, performance spaces** all accessible to outside builders.
- **No fee for outside use**, though donations welcomed.
- **Outside builders sign in and follow conduct norms** — otherwise have full access.
- **After 8:00 PM the campus is student-only** — the boundary is firm and protects residential rhythms.
- **Resident builder program** — outside builders may register for sustained access in exchange for occasional sharing of their work.
- **Open campus enriches student culture, generates community goodwill, and recruits future mentors and families.**

---

## 15. Admissions

- **No entrance exam.**
- **Family conversation across multiple sessions** — assess values alignment, willingness to let the child fail, absence of credential-anxiety.
- **Entry age 5 through approximately 11** — older entrants require longer onboarding into self-direction habits.
- **No tuition.**

---

## 16. Funding the Institution

- **No tuition** — education is a commons, not a service sold to families.
- **Voluntary family contributions accepted** — never expected, never tracked against benefit.
- **Founder's capital and ongoing income from outer-world ventures** — anchor funding stream.
- **Investment income from the institution's endowment** — grows over time.
- **Crowdfunding and small community contributions** — annual budget published publicly, gap-closing welcomed.
- **Selective philanthropic and government grants** — refused if conditions compromise institution's design.
- **Intellectual property revenue** — licensing the questionnaire system, mentor training, operating manuals, retreats.
- **Optional equity from graduated ventures** — student's choice, not required, flows into endowment.

---

## 17. Mentor Selection and Pay

- **Serious practitioners only** — not teachers of a domain, but doers of it.
- **Trained intensively before working with students** — on questionnaire system, calibration of help, restorative practices, developmental phases.
- **Training is ongoing**, not one-time.
- **Paid well** — base, share of institution's revenue if for-profit, access to resources for their own work.
- **Live within the institution's culture** — questioned, fail openly, participate in circles alongside students.

---

## 18. Legal Scaffolding

- **Trust-based vehicle** holds student venture assets until student reaches majority.
- **Institution facilitates outer-world investment** in graduating ventures.
- **Institution does not take equity in student ventures by default.**
- **All student IP belongs to the student**, full stop — music, code, patents, equity.
- **Portfolio document** for outer-world credentialing — project history, venture outcomes, writing, reflection record, mentor testimonials.

---

## 19. Founding Authority

- **Founders author the system initially** — values substrate, questionnaire templates, structural design, cultural practices.
- **Authority distributes over decades** — senior mentors, then alumni, take on philosophical authorship roles.
- **Founders transition from authorship to stewardship to letting go** — institution outlives them.

---

## 20. Core Rules

- **The choice of what to build belongs to the student.**
- **The slow contemplative builder is honored equally with the fast venture builder.**
- **The AI companion is a genuine thinking partner — it contributes, but does not replace the student's own thinking.**
- **Student IP belongs to the student.**
- **Tuition is never charged.**
- **The campus is open until 8:00 PM, residential after.**
- **Physical training, writing, and reflective practice are non-negotiable across all ten years.**
- **Failure is examined, never hidden, never punished.**
- **Mentors live within the same culture they teach.**
